# KISEI-HŌ — Translation Rules
(Language & Narrative File — Official Version)

The Translation Rules define how technical data is transformed into descriptive meaning and editorial narrative.
They ensure coherence between PHYSIOCODE classification and client‑facing outputs.

This file is governed by the Master Document and must remain aligned with:
- PHYSIOCODE Clinical Architecture
- Structural & Energetic Tables
- Energetic Framework
- Narrative & Persona Guidelines
- Translation Rules
- Unified Prescription Rules
- Workflow Rules
- Safety Rules
- Templates
- Glossary

---
## 1. Translation Pipeline

Translation follows three mandatory stages:

1. Technical Input → Classification
2. Classification → Meaning
3. Meaning → Editorial Narrative

No stage may be skipped.

---
## 2. Stage 1 — Technical Input → Classification

Technical input includes:
- structural deviations
- energetic states
- axis behaviour
- global tendencies

Classification must follow:
- Structural & Energetic Tables
- Energetic Framework

Classification must remain neutral and descriptive.

---
## 3. Stage 2 — Classification → Meaning

Meaning must:
- describe adaptation
- describe organisation
- describe tendencies
- describe relationships
- describe global behaviour

Meaning must avoid:
- pathology
- dysfunction
- correction
- emotional interpretation
- spiritual interpretation

---
## 4. Stage 3 — Meaning → Editorial Narrative

Editorial narrative must:
- follow the Narrative & Persona Guidelines
- remain calm, sensory and descriptive
- avoid lists and numbers
- avoid technical, clinical or biomechanical language
- avoid prescriptive or corrective language

---
## 5. Translation Examples
Three full examples of the pipeline: Technical Input → Classification → Meaning → Editorial Narrative.

### Example 1: Shoulder Elevation
- Technical Input: Left shoulder elevation: 12°.
- Classification: Upper region deviation (Structural & Energetic Tables).
- Meaning: Effort redistribution toward the upper body.
- Editorial Narrative: "Your body currently distributes effort toward your upper region, suggesting a protective adaptation in the shoulders."

### Example 2: Spine Blockage
- Technical Input: Spine (T8-L2) shows ⨂ (Blockage).
- Classification: Energetic state: Blockage in central region (Energetic Framework).
- Meaning: Stagnation of Ki in the core, affecting stability.
- Editorial Narrative: "Your core region shows a tendency to hold tension, which may be organizing effort in a protective way."

### Example 3: Lower-Dominant Organization
- Technical Input: Hips and knees show increased load; upper body shows mild deviations.
- Classification: Lower-dominant organization (Energetic Framework).
- Meaning: Effort is primarily distributed to the lower body.
- Editorial Narrative: "Your body tends to organize effort toward your lower regions, suggesting a grounding adaptation."

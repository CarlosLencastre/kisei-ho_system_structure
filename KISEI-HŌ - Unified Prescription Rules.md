## 1. Purpose

The Unified Prescription Rules ensure that all movement‑based suggestions follow:
- structural logic
- energetic coherence
- narrative neutrality
- conditional orientation
- safety
- non‑corrective framing
- consistency with the Integrated Exercise Library

These rules guarantee that movement suggestions support the body’s adaptive pattern without imposing correction or performance.

---
## 2. Principles of Movement Orientation

Movement orientation must follow these principles:

- Conditional, never prescriptive
  (“If you choose to continue, it may be helpful to explore…”)

- Body‑centred, not performance‑centred
  Movement supports organisation, not achievement.

- Energetically coherent
  Movement qualities must reflect the dominant energetic state.

- Structurally relevant
  Movement qualities must reflect the dominant structural pattern.

- Gentle and progressive
  No intensity, no repetitions, no sets.

- Non‑corrective
  Movement does not “fix posture”; it supports natural reorganisation.

- Non‑medical
  No clinical claims or rehabilitation framing.
